Finding My Inner Self During the Pandemic

  • Contributor:

    Chairene Chidozie

  • 01 May 2021

The night of December 31st 2019, was a special night for everyone. A new decade awaited us all, which many associated with new beginnings and unrealistic promises. I was heavily affected by the latter.

As I savored my prosecco rosé, I wrote a letter to my future self, where I outlined everything I wanted to accomplish by the end of 2020. What began as a waltz between the current version of myself and the powerful Michelle Obama—whom for me, like many others is a constant source of inspiration. Ended with a list of dull goals, any young lady who had been heartbroken, gained a few pounds, or a victim of consumerism could have composed. Every goal I had written had a theme of self-transformation with the intent of receiving external validation. Little did I know that my incapability of smartly setting goals would not define 2020. Instead, it would be defined by a global pandemic that not only interrupted a normative routine/lifestyle for so many, but it further amplified racial and social injustices through a racially divisive lens.

When I first caught wind of the pandemic and how my busy yet unfulfilling life was about to change drastically, I was delighted. It meant that I had an excuse to not keep up with my social circles. My relief screamed 'privileged', whilst many worried about job and food security; I embraced that the urgency of meeting my commitments were gradually fading.

This time of solitude provided by the pandemic revealed the urgency to relearn how to live for myself. Without a doubt, I believed composing a new list of goals was the best way to approach this successfully. However, this time, I decided to write down everything that made me happy and enjoy life more.

With the intent of being as honest as possible, my list illuminated that I was a food-loving, reality tv admirer who cared about making an impact on the world. I tackled this premature epitaph of myself by doing each of the things on my list within one week. Other than this approach being far from fulfilling, it was also draining and exhausting.

As I enjoyed my banana brownies and listened to a podcast by Women of Impact, I realised that the past week I had been forcing myself to do 'joyful' things, making those things draining, as it felt like quite a superficial approach to this drastic change. My unsuccessful approach did not mean that I was out of touch with my feelings; I just needed a break to breathe and come to terms with myself.

Image: My What Makes Me Happy List

It was time to integrate my essential self. The focus here was to review my life and happiness triggers. After doing so, I concluded that my 'what makes me happy' list was at surface level reflective of my “goals”, that I wanted to achieve. But looking back at it - this list like many others - reminded me of how unnecessary and how out of touch those goals were with my true feelings, as I was suffering from emotional exhaustion at the time of writing it. By forcing myself to activate my happy hormones, I was indirectly punishing myself for being sad. If this year has taught me anything, it is that it is okay not to be okay. Sometimes permitting yourself to let out your sadness is the best way to trigger your happiness.
