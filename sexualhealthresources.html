Sexual Health Services

General disclaimer: The stigma surrounding STD’s/STI’s and getting tested for them stems both from general misinformation in sexual health education, and in some cases the complete lack of sexual health education in our contemporary setting. We still lack accessible information and education surrounding sexual health, and it’s vital to start openly talking about the normalcy of getting tested, whether you are sexually active or not. Getting tested can seem so daunting and anxiety inducing, and from personal experience it always helps to ask close friends for support/advice. Yet sexual health clinics are there to provide you with all the correct information about contraception, getting tested, consensual sex, and so much more. These clinics provide a safe space for you to ask any sort of question, where you can feel heard, and most importantly safe. Sexual health clinics also provide a space where you can break down stigmas about STI’s/STD’s, and choose the best way for you to have safe and protected sex.

Not all clinics worldwide provide these safe spaces, but with this list we aim to highlight the resources that provide these services in the Netherlands.

  • The Centre for Sexual Health : Offers tests and treatments for STI free of charge. A referral by your GP (huisarts) is not necessary. This centre also supports young adults with sexual problems as well as individuals who are victims of sexual abuse.
  • STI outpatient clinic of GGD Amsterdam : Is a supplementary facility for general practitioner, for free examination, and if necessary, treatment of STIs (sexually transmitted infection). Young people up to the age of 25 can make an appointment for free sexual assistance (Sense).
  • SOA Student Arts : They are on a mission to declare every student in the Netherlands STD-free. By providing students the opportunity to orders tests online. That means students test themselves for STD’s at home and no longer have to go to the doctor or GGD.
  • Testalize.me : Offers an online alternative that allows individuals (including tourists) to request an STD test online and perform it in the comfort of their own home. There are no waiting lists, and the results are revealed within 48 hours. In addition, everything is completely anonymous, and their tests are a lot cheaper than those conducted by a GP.
